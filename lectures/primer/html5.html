HTML5, The New HTML

HTML didn’t really change all that much over 10 years (1999–2009). However, in 2004, the W3C’s XHTML 2 (non-backwards compatible with existing web content or even previous versions of HTML) was met with opposition by The Web Hypertext Application Technology Working Group (WHATWG) — formed by members of Opera, Apple, and Mozilla to extend the life of HTML with HTML5.
